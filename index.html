About the Department

The Department of Information & Communication Technology at MUT is committed to providing high-quality education and research in the field of ICT. Our programs are designed to equip students with the skills and knowledge needed to thrive in the rapidly evolving technology sector.

We offer a range of qualifications from diploma to advanced diploma levels, with specializations in software development and communication networks.

Explore Our Programs

Why Study ICT?

Every company needs an ICT specialist who not only understands the technology behind computers and mobile devices but also understands the needs of the company and the workforce. An ICT professional is in charge of the physical hardware like computers, printers, and copiers, as well as software, communication and connection systems, and online platforms.

They also know how to use these technologies to streamline business processes. An ICT specialist designs, maintains, and services the systems used to generate, store, send and retrieve data.

Career Opportunities

There are a wide range of careers in this field, ranging from librarians to the military. You can become a computer service technician, a programmer, data technician, web developer, systems analyst or IT engineer. Most companies of any size will also employ an ICT specialist to maintain their hardware and software and offer technological support to staff, while there are jobs within companies that specialize in offering ICT services.

Program Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this program, graduates will be able to:

  • Analyze, design and implement software solutions for business problems
  • Design and implement computer networks and infrastructure
  • Apply systems analysis and design principles to ICT solutions
  • Develop and maintain databases for organizational information systems
  • Demonstrate professional and ethical conduct in ICT practice
Curriculum Structure

The program consists of the following core modules:

Year 1
  • Programming Fundamentals
  • Computer Networks I
  • Database Fundamentals
  • Business Communication
  • Mathematics for IT
Year 2
  • Object-Oriented Programming
  • Web Development
  • Systems Analysis and Design
  • Network Administration
  • Database Management Systems
Year 3
  • Advanced Programming
  • IT Project Management
  • Information Security
  • Work Integrated Learning
  • Specialization Electives

Program Overview

The Extended Curriculum Program (ECP) is designed for students who show potential but may need additional academic support. The first year serves as a foundation year to strengthen key skills before students join the mainstream diploma program in their second year.

Foundation Year Benefits
  • Smaller class sizes for personalized attention
  • Additional academic support in Mathematics and English
  • Introduction to key IT concepts at a more gradual pace
  • Study skills development for university success
  • Smoother transition to the demands of higher education
Curriculum Structure
Foundation Year
  • Academic Literacy
  • Mathematics for IT
  • Introduction to Computers
  • Basic Programming Concepts
  • Communication Skills
  • Study Methods
Years 2-4

After successfully completing the foundation year, students join the standard Diploma in IT program with the same curriculum as shown in the regular diploma program.

Postgraduate Studies in ICT

The Department of ICT offers a range of postgraduate opportunities for students wishing to advance their knowledge and research capabilities in specialized areas of information and communication technology.

Available Programs
  • Bachelor of Technology (B-Tech) in IT - NQF Level 7
  • Postgraduate Diploma in ICT - NQF Level 8
  • Master of Information Technology - NQF Level 9
  • Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in ICT - NQF Level 10
Research Focus Areas

Our postgraduate students engage in cutting-edge research across several key areas:

Technical Focus Areas
  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
  • Data Science and Big Data Analytics
  • Cybersecurity and Digital Forensics
  • Cloud Computing and Distributed Systems
  • Internet of Things (IoT) and Smart Systems
Applied Focus Areas
  • ICT for Development
  • Educational Technology
  • Health Informatics
  • E-Commerce and Digital Business
  • Human-Computer Interaction
Admission Requirements
  • B-Tech: Diploma in IT or equivalent with 60% average
  • Postgrad Diploma: B-Tech or equivalent degree
  • Master's: Honors degree or equivalent with 65% average
  • PhD: Master's degree in relevant field with research experience

International students must meet equivalent qualifications and demonstrate English proficiency.
